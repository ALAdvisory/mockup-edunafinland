### Founders Section

**OLGA SAGHAR - Co-Founder & Special Education Expert**

With over 15 years working in Finland and internationally, including the MENA region, Olga currently supports 25+ educators in private early years centres and preschools. She holds a Master's in Early Childhood Special Education and Psychology with specialized training in speech therapy. Olga especially helps educators working with bilingual and multilingual children, spotting early signs of additional support needs before they become bigger challenges.

Her strength is early intervention and building bridges between educators and families. When communication gets complex or cultural differences create uncertainty, Olga brings clarity. She turns complicated assessments into practical next steps. Educators gain confidence. Families feel heard. Children get the support they need, when they need it.

---

**PÄIVI PEKKALA - Co-Founder & Inclusion Specialist**

Over 25 years, Päivi has supported hundreds of educators working with children who have ADHD and autism. She holds five degrees in education and brings personal experience as a parent navigating these same challenges. Päivi especially helps educators facing sensory overwhelm, emotional regulation difficulties, and complex family situations through her expertise in music-based interventions and team collaboration.

Her approach is simple: listen first, then solve together. Educators gain practical skills to handle situations that once felt overwhelming. Parents find someone who truly understands. Kids feel safe. Päivi helps you build the classroom you've always wanted, one small change at a time.

### Finnish Special Education Framework Section
**Headline:** Finnish Special Education Framework

**Description:** These principles shaped decades of successful inclusive education in Finland. They work.

**Principles:**

1. **Learning Through Play & Curiosity**
   Children explore and discover through joyful, hands-on experiences that spark natural curiosity.

2. **All-Day Pedagogy**
   Learning happens everywhere — not only in lessons, but in play, routines, and relationships.

3. **Equality & Inclusion as Foundation**
   Every child deserves the same opportunities and support to thrive, regardless of their needs.

4. **Collaboration & Reflection**
   Teachers work together, share insights, and continuously reflect on their practice.

5. **Trust in Teachers**
   Educators are respected professionals with the autonomy to make thoughtful decisions.

6. **Shared Responsibility**
   Education is a collaborative effort between teachers, care givers, and the community.
